RECIPE


    • 1 medium head of green cabbage
    • 2 tablespoons butter or vegetable oil
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 apple, peeled, cored, and diced
    • 1/2 cup chicken or vegetable broth
    • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
    • 2 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Caraway seeds (optional, for flavor)


PROCEDURE


    Prepare the Cabbage:
    • Remove the tough outer leaves of the cabbage, then cut it into quarters. Remove the core and slice the cabbage thinly.

    Sauté the Onion and Garlic:
    • In a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven, melt the butter or heat the oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.

    Add the Cabbage:
    • Add the sliced cabbage to the skillet. Stir well to combine it with the onion and garlic.

    Braise the Cabbage:
    • Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and apple cider vinegar. Sprinkle the sugar over the cabbage. If desired, add a pinch of caraway seeds for a traditional flavor.
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste. Give everything a good stir.

    Cover and Simmer:
    • Cover the skillet or Dutch oven with a lid and reduce the heat to low. Allow the cabbage to simmer gently for about 30-40 minutes, or until it becomes tender. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking.

    Add the Apple:
    • About halfway through the cooking time, add the diced apple to the cabbage mixture. The apple will add a hint of sweetness and flavor.

    Taste and Adjust:
    • Taste the braised cabbage and adjust the seasonings if needed. You can add more sugar or vinegar to achieve your desired balance of sweetness and acidity.

    Serve:
    • Once the cabbage is tender and the flavors have melded together, remove it from the heat. Serve the braised cabbage as a delicious side dish alongside traditional German main courses like schnitzel or sausages.
