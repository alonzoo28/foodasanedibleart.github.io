RECIPE


    • 2 pounds (900g) beef stew meat, cut into chunks
    • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 2 carrots, sliced
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    • 1 bottle (750ml) of red wine (such as Burgundy)
    • 2 cups beef broth
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 pound (450g) mushrooms, quartered
    • 20-30 pearl onions, peeled
    • Fresh parsley for garnish


PROCEDURE


    • In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the bacon and cook until it's crispy. Remove the bacon and set it aside.
    • In the same pot, add the beef chunks and brown them on all sides. Remove the beef and set it aside with the bacon.
    • Add the chopped onions and carrots to the pot. Cook for about 5 minutes until they start to soften. Then add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
    • Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to coat them. This will help thicken the stew.
    • Return the bacon and beef to the pot. Pour in the entire bottle of red wine, beef broth, bay leaves, dried thyme, salt, and pepper.
    • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 2-3 hours, or until the beef is tender and the sauce has thickened. Stir occasionally.
    • While the stew is simmering, in a separate skillet, sauté the mushrooms in a bit of butter or olive oil until they're browned. Set them aside.
    • In the same skillet, add the pearl onions and cook until they're browned and slightly caramelized. Set them aside.
    • Once the beef is tender, remove the bay leaves. Add the sautéed mushrooms and pearl onions to the pot. Simmer for an additional 15-20 minutes.
    • Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can also add a splash of red wine if desired.
    • Serve your Boeuf Bourguignon hot, garnished with fresh parsley. It pairs wonderfully with crusty bread, mashed potatoes, or noodles.
